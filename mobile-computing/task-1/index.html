Introduction

Background

  • Raising fish reduces stress and blood pressure; helps treat adolescents with diabetes
  • COVID-19 pandemic led to increased popularity of ornamental fish keeping
  • Fish farming businesses often encounter challenges in adequate fish care

Challenges

  • Insufficient staff or caretakers for aquarium maintenance
  • Difficulty maintaining appropriate water quality for each fish species
  • Poor water quality leads to fish fatalities and business losses

"For fish to survive for a long time, there are many factors such as water temperature, turbidity, TDS, pH, ORP, EC, DO, nitrate and phosphate."

Why IoT and AI?

Internet of Things and neural networks can efficiently monitor and control water quality, automate water replacement, and predict ideal conditions for fish survival and growth.

Water Quality Parameters

Temperature

  • Fish are cold-blooded animals
  • Body temperature/metabolism changes with water temperature
  • Affects growth and breeding directly

pH Level

  • Concentration of hydrogen ions (H+)
  • pH 7.0 is neutral, below is acidic, above is alkaline
  • Suitable pH for fish: between 6.5 and 9.0

Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

  • Oxygen from atmosphere and plants dissolves in water
  • Minimum DO for many fish: 4.0-5.0 mg/L
  • Essential for fish respiration and metabolism

Turbidity

  • Cloudiness in water from suspended solids
  • Affects light penetration and water clarity
  • Can clog fish respiratory system
  • Measured in Nephelometric Turbidity Units (NTU)

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

  • Concentration of dissolved organic and inorganic substances
  • Can cause toxicity through increased salinity
  • Affects fish metabolism and reproduction

Nitrate Ion

  • Produced from nitrogen cycle in aquarium
  • Forms from fish waste and uneaten food
  • High levels decrease blood hemoglobin, affecting oxygen intake
  • Regular water replacement reduces nitrate levels

The Nitrogen Cycle

Fish waste and uneaten food → Ammonia (NH₃) → Nitrites (NO₂⁻) → Nitrates (NO₃⁻). Water changes help remove nitrates which can be toxic to fish at high levels.

Data Acquisition

  • Collected data from different ornamental fish farms
  • Focus on five favorite tropical fish in Thailand: Angelfish, Goldfish, Guppy, Platy, and Sumatran Tiger Barb
  • 1,592 records with 10 features and 1 class (water quality)
  • Features: fish kind, pH, DO, TDS, nitrate, temperature, turbidity, etc.
  • Output class: water quality (good, poor)

SMOTE for Imbalanced Data

Synthetic Minority Over-Sampling Technique helps resynthesize data by:

  • Growing dataset size with small amount of class data
  • Making dataset consistent with largest class
  • Setting k=5 nearest neighbors and randomSeed=1
  • Increasing dataset from 1,592 to 5,469 records (400% SMOTE)

Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Network

Key MLPNN characteristics:

  • Three layers: input, hidden, and output layers
  • Processing elements connected by weights for classification
  • Parameters: six hidden layers, sigmoid activation function
  • 500 epochs, batch size=auto, early stopping=true
  • Adaptive moment optimization (Adam) with learning rate=0.005
  • Web API created with Flask framework for model deployment

Model Evaluation Methods

  • Training accuracy and Mean Square Error (MSE)
  • 10-fold cross-validation process
  • Confusion matrix for accuracy, sensitivity, and specificity
  • Comparison of original and SMOTE-enhanced models

IoT System Implementation

IoT System Architecture

The infrastructure of automatic aquarium system

Hardware Components

  • ESP32 DevKitC: Microcontroller with Wi-Fi capability
  • Sensors: DS18B20 (temperature), pH, DO, TDS, Nitrate ion, LDR (turbidity)
  • Water Control: 12V-water pump, 2-way solenoid valve, ultrasonic sensor, horizontal water level sensor
  • Environmental Control: 5V-LED tube light, 6V-12V air pumps, 12V cooling fans, water heater
  • Feeding System: 12V-step motor with driver, force sensor resistor (FSR)
  • Other: Power supply, relays, microSD card module

Custom Turbidity Sensor

Developed using white LED and LDR sensor:

  • Clear water (0 NTU): Vout = 4.56V
  • Maximum turbidity (2,982 NTU): Vout = 2.50V
  • Quadratic equation derived for NTU calculation
  • NTU = -702.705 × Vout² + 3,513.526 × Vout - 1,409.908

Automatic Control Functions

1. Water Quality Monitoring

Temperature and DO measured every 5 minutes; pH, TDS, nitrate, and turbidity every 30 minutes. Values sent to server for analysis.

2. Temperature Control

Cooling fans or water heater activated based on temperature readings to maintain optimal conditions.

3. Air Pump Control

Air pumps automatically adjust to increase DO levels when readings are low.

4. Water Replacement

Automatic water replacement when quality is poor, using water pump, ultrasonic sensor, and solenoid valve.

5. Feeding System

Scheduled feeding with quantity monitoring using step motor and FSR sensor.

6. Notification System

LINE Notify sends alerts for critical events; mobile app provides real-time monitoring and control.

Software Architecture

  • Arduino IDE for ESP32 controller programming (C++)
  • Apache web server for communication between smartphone and ESP32
  • MySQL database for data storage
  • Bootstrap framework for responsive mobile application
  • Flask framework for MLPNN model API
  • LINE Notify for push notifications

Key Findings

  • 400% SMOTE + MLPNN produced highest accuracy (97.31%)
  • Model stopped training at 218th epoch with lowest MSE (0.0154)
  • Overall system accuracy: 99.89%
  • External factors (weather, internet connection) slightly affected accuracy
  • Testing and installation at fifteen fish farms for thirty days confirmed reliability
